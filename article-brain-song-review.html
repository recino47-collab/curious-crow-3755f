The Science: How Binaural Beats Work

Binaural beats aren't new—they were discovered in 1839. Here's how they work:

When you play two slightly different frequencies through stereo headphones (e.g., 100 Hz in the left ear and 107 Hz in the right), your brain perceives a "beat" at the difference frequency (7 Hz in this case). This beat frequency can influence your brain's electrical activity.

Different frequency ranges correspond to different brainwave states:

  • Delta (0.5-4 Hz): Deep sleep, restoration
  • Theta (4-8 Hz): Light sleep, meditation, creativity
  • Alpha (8-12 Hz): Relaxed focus, calm awareness
  • Beta (12-30 Hz): Active thinking, concentration, alertness

The Brain Song uses alpha and beta frequencies to induce a state of relaxed focus—the sweet spot for memory consolidation and cognitive performance. Multiple studies in neuroscience journals have shown that binaural beat exposure correlates with measurable improvements in attention and working memory.
